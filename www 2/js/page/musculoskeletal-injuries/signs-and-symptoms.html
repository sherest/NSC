Signs and Symptoms of Musculoskeletal Injuries

  • Deformity of area or angulation of extremity (compare to opposite extremity)
  • Pain and tenderness
  • Feeling or sound of bone ends grating
  • Swelling
  • Skin discoloration: bruising or cyanosis
  • Bone ends exposed in an open wound
  • Joint locked into position
  • Abnormal sensation (numbness, tingling)
  • Impaired function or circulation
  • Inability to move the area
  • Temperature difference from the opposite extremity

Emergency Care for Musculoskeletal Injuries

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. After controlling any life threats, let the patient remain in a position of comfort.
  3. Cover open wounds with a sterile dressing.
  4. Put a cold pack on an area of painful, swollen, deformed extremity, except on an open fracture, with damp cloth barrier between skin and cold.
  5. Do not try to replace bones protruding from a wound.
  6. Stabilize an injured extremity manually, or have the patient manually support the area.
    • Support above and below the injury.
    • Pad the area.
    • If in doubt, manually stabilize the injury.
  7. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  8. If appropriate, splint the extremity.
  9. Apply RICE as appropriate: rest, ice, compression, elevation.
