Helicopter Landing Zone

  • Follow local protocols and state requirements
  • Landing zone should be:
    • A flat area free of obstructions such as trees, power lines and buildings
    • At safe distance from emergency scene.
    • At least 100 feet by 100 feet.
    • An area free of loose objects, stones, and dirt that will be blown by rotor wash.
  • Remove objects that pose a risk if blown.
  • Mark corners of landing zone with clearly visible objects, such as traffic cones or flags.
    • At night, mark corners with strobe lights, chemical light sticks or flares (if no fire risk).
    • Do not point vehicle headlights toward center of zone.
    • Police vehicles may be used to mark corners with emergency lights.
  • Communicate with the pilot and describe landing zone and corner markings and any nearby obstructions or hazards that may not be obvious. The pilot approves or rejects the landing zone.
