Poison Control Centers

Emergency Care for Swallowed Poison

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Patient’s condition may change rapidly; care depends on the patient’s condition.
  3. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  4. For an unresponsive patient:
    • Ensure EMS has been activated.
    • Check for normal breathing and provide BLS if needed.
    • Put a breathing, unresponsive patient in the recovery position, because of the risk of vomiting.
    • Continue to monitor breathing and vital signs. 
  5. For a responsive patient:
    • For a patient with signs of a life-threatening condition (including sleepiness, seizures, difficulty breathing, vomiting), activate EMS or arrange for EMS transport and provide basic life support (BLS) as needed.
    • If mouth or lips are burned by a corrosive chemical, rinse mouth with cold water (without swallowing).
    • Follow local protocol to call Poison Control Centers: 800-222-1222 or medical direction, and follow their instructions.
    • Do not give any substance to eat or drink unless instructed by PCC or medical direction.  
    • Do not follow instructions on household product labels.
    • Depending on the poison, you may be directed:
      • To dilute the poison by having the patient drink water or milk.
      • To have the patient drink a solution of activated charcoal. Follow local protocol and PCC instructions.
