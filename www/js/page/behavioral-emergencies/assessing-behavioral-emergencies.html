Assessing Behavioral Emergencies

  • Perform the standard assessment.
  • Assess mental status by observing:

      • - General appearance

      - Posture and gait

      - Actions

      - Speech

      - Orientation for time, person and place

      - Mood, thought processes, perception, judgment, memory and attention

  • Assess potential for violence:

      • - Check scene for potential weapons.

      - In the history, ask family members and bystanders about any history of aggression or combativeness.

      - Observe patient’s posture: whether in a position that threatens self or others (clenched fists, holding lethal objects, tense muscles).

      - Observe what patient says and how: whether yelling or verbally threatening harm to self or others or seems to be hallucinating or yelling at someone not present.

      - Consider the warning signs for suicide.

      - Observe patient’s physical activity: Watch for movement toward you and quick, irregular movements.

      - Observe patient’s emotional mood, such as being agitated, uncontrollably elated, depressed, uncontrollably angry or kicking or throwing things.

      - Consider whether patient is manifesting bizarre thinking and behavior, such as hallucinations or paranoia.
