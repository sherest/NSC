Controlling External Bleeding

  1. Wearing medical examination gloves, put a sterile dressing on the wound and apply firm pressure directly on the point of bleeding for about 5 minutes.
  2. With large gaping wounds, apply sterile gauze and apply direct pressure with full hand if needed to control bleeding.
  3. Re-evaluate the bleeding: If it continues, put another dressing or cloth pad on top of the first and keep applying pressure.

  5. If you are unable to control bleeding with direct pressure alone, follow local protocol to use a hemostatic dressing, or a tourniquet on an extremity.
  6. Continually monitor controlled bleeding to identify recurring bleeding.
  7. If appropriate, treat for shock.
