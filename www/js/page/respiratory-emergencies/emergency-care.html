Emergency Care for an Asthma Attack

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Follow local protocol to assist the patient in using his or her medication (usually a metered dose inhaler) when:
    • Patient confirms it is an asthma attack.
    • Patient identifies the inhaler as his or her prescribed asthma medication.
    • Patient cannot self-administer the medication.
  3. Help the patient rest and sit in a position for easiest breathing.
  4. Patient may use an inhaler again if needed.
  5. Follow local protocol to give oxygen if available and you are trained.
