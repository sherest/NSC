Inadequate Breathing

  1. Inadequate breathing is breathing too slowly (less than 10 breaths/minute) or too weakly.
  2. Patient likely has altered mental status and may have cyanotic appearance.
  3. Patient needs ventilations.
  4. Do not wait for respiratory arrest before providing ventilations for an adult who is not breathing adequately.
  5. An infant or child may have a pulse of 60 or higher but still be breathing inadequately and need ventilation.

Emergency Care for Respiratory Distress

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Help the patient rest in a position of easiest breathing (often sitting up).
  3. Ask the patient about any prescribed medicine, and help the patient take it if needed.
  4. Be prepared to give BLS, because respiratory distress may progress to respiratory arrest.
  5. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  6. Use suction as needed to maintain the airway.
  7. Because respiratory distress in an infant or a child may rapidly progress to arrest, act quickly when an infant or a child is having problems breathing.
