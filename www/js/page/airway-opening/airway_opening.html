In unresponsive patient, open airway with head tilt–chin lift if no potential spinal injury, or the jaw thrust if spinal injury suspected.

Head Tilt–Chin Lift

  • Tilt the head back and lift the chin to move tongue away from opening into throat.
  • Use your fingers, not your thumb, to lift the chin. Do not press deeply into soft tissues of chin.
  • Ensure the mouth stays open.

Jaw Thrust

With possible spinal injury, do not tilt head back. Only lift jaw upward, using both hands.

  • Jaw thrust is more difficult and fatiguing but is effective.
  • If the lips close, open lower lip with your gloved thumb.
  • When in doubt about a possible spinal injury, use the jaw thrust.
  • If jaw thrust does not successfully open the airway, then switch to head tilt–chin lift.
