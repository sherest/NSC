Bites

Care for Animal Bites

  1. Follow general principles of wound care.
  2. Clean the wound with large amounts of warm or room-temperature water with or without soap (except when bleeding severely).
  3. Control bleeding.
  4. Cover wound with sterile dressing and bandage.
  5. Ensure patient sees health care provider as soon as possible.
  6. Do not try to catch any animal that may have rabies. Note its appearance and describe it to health care provider.
  7. All animal bites should be reported to local animal control officers or law enforcement personnel.

Care for Other Bites and Stings

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Have patient lie down and stay calm. (Do not move patient unless absolutely necessary.)
  3. Wash bite wound with large amounts of warm or room-temperature water with or without soap.
  4. Remove jewelry or tight clothing before swelling begins.
  5. Do not put a tourniquet on patient. With snake bite, wrap extremity with compression bandage.
  6. Do not cut the wound open to try to drain or suck out venom.
  7. Remove a stinger and venom sac from skin by scraping it away gently with a piece of plastic like a credit card (not a knife blade).
  8. Observe for signs or symptoms of severe allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling in other areas, anxiety, nausea or vomiting), and treat for shock.
  9. If patient is allergic to bee or wasp stings and has an emergency epinephrine auto-injector, follow local protocol to assist with the medication. (See here)
  10. Place unresponsive patient in recovery position, monitor breathing and vital signs, and give BLS as needed.
