Fainting

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. If fainting is anticipated, have the patient sit or lie down.
  3. Injury may result if a fainting patient falls – try to catch the patient and gently lower to the floor.
  4. Unless the patient may have spinal or leg injury, lay the patient down and raise legs about 6 to 12 inches.
  5. Loosen constricting clothing.
  6. Check for possible injuries caused by falling.
  7. Be prepared to provide BLS if needed.
  8. Place a patient who is breathing normally but remains unresponsive in the recovery position.
  9. Reassure the patient as he or she recovers.
  10. Do not pour or splash water on the patient’s face (could be aspirated into the lungs).
  11. Do not use ammonia inhalants or similar remedies to arouse the patient.
  12. Activate EMS if the patient does not regain responsiveness soon or faints repeatedly. Always call EMS for older adults, people with heart disease, and pregnant women.
