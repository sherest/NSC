Cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR)

Call First vs. Call Fast

  • When alone with an adult patient, immediately call first for help and an AED, as soon as the patient is identified as unresponsive, before providing CPR, except as noted below.
  • For infant or child (more likely to have airway obstruction), call fast for help and an AED after providing 5 cycles of CPR (about 2 minutes). For unresponsive patients in cardiac arrest due to likely asphyxial arrest (drowning patient), call fast. Give 5 cycles of CPR (about 2 minutes) before stopping to call for help.

1-Rescuer CPR

  1. Activate the emergency response system. Determine that the unresponsive patient is not breathing normally and has no pulse.
  2. Expose the chest. Put your hand(s) in correct position for chest compressions.
  3. Give 30 chest compressions hard and fast at least 2 inches deep in an adult (but not more than 2.4 inches) and at least ⅓ the depth of the chest in an infant (about 1½ inches) or child (about 2 inches) at a rate of 100-120 per minute. Count aloud for a steady, fast rate: “1, 2, 3...” Then give 2 breaths.
  4. Open the airway and give 2 rescue breaths, each over 1 second, to cause a visible chest rise. (If the first breath does not go in, reposition the patient’s head and try again; if the second breath still does not go in, give choking care.)
  5. Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 ventilations.
  6. Continue CPR until:
    • The patient is breathing normally and has a pulse
    • An AED is brought to the scene and is ready to use
    • Personnel with more training arrive and take over
    • if the patient starts breathing normally and has a pulse but is unresponsive, put the patient in the recovery position and monitor breathing and pulse.
  7. When an AED arrives and is ready to be used, start the AED sequence for a patient who is not breathing normally and has no pulse.

2-Rescuer CPR for Adults and Children

  1. At patient’s head, Rescuer 1 checks for unresponsiveness, normal breathing, and a pulse simultaneously for no longer than 10 seconds. Rescuer 2 ensures AED has been summoned. Rescuer 2 locates the site for chest compressions.
  2. Rescuer 1 indicates, “No pulse.” Rescuer 2 gives 30 compressions for adult (15 for a child) at rate of 100-120 per minute, then pauses.
  3. Rescuer 1 gives 2 breaths.
  4. Rescuers continue cycles of 30 compressions in adult (15 in child) and 2 breaths for about 2 minutes (or after 5 cycles of compressions and ventilations at a ratio of 30:2) before switching compressor and ventilator roles in less than 5 seconds.
  5. Rescuers continue CPR until:
    • The patient is breathing normally and has a pulse
    • An AED is brought to the scene and is ready to use
    • Help arrives and takes over
    • if patient starts breathing normally and has pulse but is unresponsive, put in recovery position and monitor breathing and pulse.
  6. When AED arrives and is ready to be used, start AED sequence for patient who is not breathing normally and has no pulse.

2-Rescuer CPR for Infants

  • Rescuer giving compressions places thumbs of both hands together in correct position on sternum (just below nipple line).
  • Fingers of both hands encircle infant’s chest.
  • Chest is compressed with both thumbs while chest is squeezed with fingers.
  1. At infant’s head, Rescuer 1 checks for normal breathing and pulse. At infant’s feet, Rescuer 2 positions hands in chest-encircling position for chest compressions with both thumbs.
  2. If pulse is absent, Rescuer 1 says, “No pulse.” Rescuer 2 gives 15 chest compressions at rate of 100-120 per minute, counting aloud for a fast, steady rate, then pauses.
  3. Rescuer 1 gives 2 breaths.
  4. Rescuers continue cycles of 15 compressions and 2 breaths for about 2 minutes before switching compressor and ventilator roles in less than 5 seconds. Rescuers continue CPR until:
    • The infant is breathing normally and has a pulse
    • An AED is brought to the scene and is ready to use
    • More advanced help arrives and takes over
    • If infant starts breathing normally but is unresponsive, hold in recovery position and monitor breathing and pulse.
  5. When AED arrives and is ready to be used, start AED sequence for infant who is not breathing normally and has no pulse.
