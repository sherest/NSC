Hypothermia

Early Signs and Symptoms

  • Shivering, numbness, lethargy, poor coordination, drowsiness, slurred speech
  • Patients often experience the “umbles”: mumbles, fumbles, stumbles.
  • Infants may have bright red skin and little energy.

Serious Later Signs and Symptoms

  • Shivering typically stops and patient may not feel cold.
  • Breathing becomes shallow, pulse slows.
  • Mental status continues to deteriorate.
  • In severe cases, the patient becomes unresponsive and may stop breathing.

Emergency Care for Hypothermia

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Remove patient from cold environment.
  3. Protect from further heat loss: Remove wet clothing and cover with a blanket.
  4. Have patient lie down, and handle gently (abrupt movement may cause arrhythmia).
  5. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  6. Do not allow patient to walk or move about.
  7. Do not give anything to eat or drink.
  8. Do not allow patient to use stimulants such as caffeine or nicotine.
  9. Do not massage the skin or extremities.
  10. Do not immerse patient in hot water or use direct heat (hot water bottle, heat lamp, heating pad) because rapid warming can cause heart problems.
  11. In an unresponsive patient, assess pulse for 30-45 seconds before starting CPR.
  12. Use AED as usual, following local protocol.
