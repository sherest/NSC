Nitroglycerin for a Cardiac Emergency

  • Increases blood flow through partially restricted arteries.
  • Generally prescribed for angina caused by narrowed coronary arteries.
  • Forms include:
      • Tablets dissolved under the tongue  
      • Tablets that dissolve in the cheek  
      • Extended-release capsules  
      • Oral sprays  
      • Extended-release patches applied to the chest
  1. Follow local protocol and the instructions on the medication to help with the drug.
  2. Patient should be seated because dizziness or fainting may occur.
  3. Do not give a drug to an unresponsive patient.
