Suctioning – Adult or Child

  1. Confirm the suction device is working.
  2. Turn patient’s head to one side and open mouth with one hand (with spinal injury, support head and turn it with body as a unit).
  3. Sweep out solids and larger amounts of fluid with index finger of other hand.
  4. Determine maximum depth of insertion by measuring catheter tip from earlobe to corner of mouth.
  5. Turn on suction, or pump handle, to create suction.
  6. Insert catheter tip carefully into patient’s mouth. Put your finger over the opening to suction, and move tip about as you withdraw it.
  7. Reposition patient’s head with airway open, and begin or resume ventilation if needed.

Suctioning – Infant

  1. Hold infant in position for suctioning, with head lower than body and turned to one side.
  2. Squeeze suction bulb first and then gently insert tip into infant’s mouth.
  3. Gradually release the bulb to create suction as you withdraw tip from the mouth.
  4. Move the bulb aside and squeeze it with tip down to empty it.
  5. Repeat steps 2-4 until the airway is clear, up to 3 times.
  6. Repeat suctioning process for each nostril.
  7. Begin or resume ventilation if needed.
