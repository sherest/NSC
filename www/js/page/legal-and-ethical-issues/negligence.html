Negligence Occurs When 4 Conditions Occur:

  • You have duty to act. Once you begin treatment, you have a duty to act.
  • You fail to give care or to meet standards of care, whether willfully (gross negligence) or unintentionally.
  • Patient suffers injury or damage, including emotional or psychological damage.
  • Patient’s injury or damage resulted from your actions or inactions, for which you can be sued (proximate cause).

Confidentiality

  • Applies to previous medical problems, physical problems, medication use and all other patient information.
  • Assessment findings and emergency care given are confidential.
  • You have an ethical responsibility to respect the patient’s right to privacy.
  • Never share patient information with others, including family members and coworkers.

Reportable Events

  • Emergency medical responders are obligated to report certain situations to appropriate authorities:

– Child, elder and spouse abuse or domestic violence  

– Certain crimes, such as gunshot and knife wounds, suspicious burns, rape and sexual assault  

– Vehicle crashes  

– Certain infectious diseases  

– Exposure to patient’s body fluids
