Signs and Symptoms of Heat Stroke

  • Skin is flushed, dry, and hot to the touch; sweating usually has stopped  
  • Fast breathing  
  • Headache, dizziness, extreme confusion, other signs of altered mental status  
  • Irrational or belligerent behavior
  • Possible convulsions or unresponsiveness

Emergency Care for Heat Stroke

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Move the patient to a cool place.
  3. Remove unnecessary clothing.
  4. Cool quickly with any means at hand:
    • Preferably Immerse the patient up to the neck in cold water (with help of second rescuer).
    • If immersion in ice cold water is not possible, place the patient in a cold shower or move to a cool area and cover as much of the body as possible with cold, wet towels.
  5. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen by non-rebreather mask if available and you are trained.
  6. Keep cooling until temperature drops to about 101°F (38.3°C).
  7. Do not apply rubbing alcohol to skin.
  8. Patient should not take pain relievers or salt tablets.
  9. Do not try to force the patient to drink liquids.
  10. If the patient is nauseous, vomiting, or experiencing diminished mental status, do not give liquids.
  11. Monitor patient and provide care as needed.
  12. Put unresponsive patient who is breathing normally in recovery position and monitor breathing and vital signs.
  13. Protect patient having convulsions from injury.
  14. Assist patient with ventilation if breathing is inadequate.
