Allergic Reactions (severe)

Early Signs and Symptoms

  • Skin flushing, itching or burning, and rash (hives)
  • Swelling around the eyes, mouth, and tongue
  • Sneezing, watery eyes and nose
  • Coughing or persistent feeling of a tickle or lump in the throat
  • Nausea, vomiting

Developing Signs and Symptoms

  • Anxiety, agitation
  • Feeling that the throat is closing and chest becoming tight
  • Rapid, weak pulse; low blood pressure
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Severe respiratory distress: coughing, wheezing, or hoarseness
  • Altered mental status
  • Severe headache
  • Weakness or dizziness
  • Pale or ashen skin or cyanosis

Emergency Care for Allergic Reactions

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Remove the allergen if possible.
  3. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  4. Help into position for easiest breathing.
  5. Follow local protocol to help responsive patient use his or her emergency epinephrine auto-injector.
