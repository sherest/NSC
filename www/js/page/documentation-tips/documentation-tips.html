Minimal Data Set

Patient Data

  • Chief complaint
  • Level of responsiveness (AVPU), mental status
  • Blood pressure (patients >3 years)
  • Skin perfusion (in children <6 years)
  • Skin color and temperature
  • Pulse
  • Respiratory rate/effort

Incident Data

  • Time incident was reported
  • Time your unit was notified
  • Time of arrival at the scene
  • Time of transfer of care to other EMS personnel
  • Time you left the scene

Patient Narrative

  • Put chief complaint in patient’s own words in quotation marks.
  • Record detailed observations, not your own conclusions.
  • Do not attempt to diagnose.
  • Include “negative” information—such as symptoms the patient denies experiencing.
  • Record the results of the physical examination in the order the examination was performed.
  • Include only pertinent information.
  • Do not use nonstandard abbreviations, slang, etc.

Correcting Errors in a Report

  • Do not attempt to erase or blot out an error.
  • If possible, use a different color ink to make corrections.
  • Draw a single line through the error, initial it, and write the correct information beside it.
  • If correcting an error at later time, add the date and time of correction with your initials.
  • If adding information originally not noted in the report, add the note along with date and time and your initials.
