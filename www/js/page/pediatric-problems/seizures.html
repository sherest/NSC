Pediatric Seizures

  • Potentially life threatening.
  • You do not need to know cause.

Emergency Care for Seizures

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Place the patient on the floor and protect from the environment.
  3. Loosen constricting clothing. Remove eyeglasses.
  4. Ask bystanders (except caretakers) to leave the area.
  5. Assure that the airway remains open.
  6. Never attempt to restrain the patient.
  7. Do not put anything in the patient’s mouth.
  8. If the patient is bluish, ensure the airway is open and give ventilations.
  9. After a seizure, place an unresponsive patient in the recovery position.
  10. Seizure patients often have significant oral secretions; be prepared to suction the airway.
  11. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  12. Report assessment findings to additional EMS personnel.
