Emergency Care for Trauma

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. In all trauma patients, use the jaw thrust to open the airway. Use the head tilt–chin lift if you cannot successfully open the airway with the jaw thrust.
  3. Suction the airway as needed.
  4. Manually stabilize the head and neck.
  5. Manually stabilize extremity injuries.
  6. Treat for shock by elevating legs, if spinal injury is not suspected, and maintaining body temperature.
  7. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  8. Ensure transport as soon as possible.
