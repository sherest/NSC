Common Signs and Symptoms of Heart Attack

  • Complaints of persistent discomfort, pressure, tightness, ache, or pain in chest
  • Complaints of pain spreading to neck, shoulders, or arms
  • Complaints of shortness of breath, either during activity or exercise or at rest, that is worse when lying down flat
  • Complaints of dizziness, lightheadedness, a feeling of impending doom
  • Pale, moist, cool skin or heavy sweating
  • Indigestion, nausea, or vomiting
  • Signs and symptoms vary considerably, and the patient may have none.
  • Women especially may experience other symptoms including shortness of breath, indigestion, nausea or vomiting, and back or jaw pain.

Emergency Care for Heart Attack

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Help the patient rest comfortably (often sitting). Loosen tight clothing. Keep the patient still.
  3. Ask if the patient is taking heart medication, and follow local protocol to help with medication. (See Nitroglycerine for a Cardiac Emergency.)
  4. Follow local protocol to encourage the patient to chew 1 adult aspirin (325 mg) or 2 to 4 low-dose baby aspirin (81 mg each) (unless allergic).
  5. Follow local protocol to administer high-concentration oxygen if available and you are trained.
  6. Stay with the patient, and be reassuring and calming.
  7. Be prepared to give CPR and use an AED if the patient becomes unresponsive and normal breathing stops.
  8. Do not let the patient eat or drink anything.
