Emergency Care for Heat Burns

  1. Perform standard patient care.
  2. Follow general principles of wound care.
  3. Stop the burning by removing heat source and any smoldering clothing.
  4. Cool the burn with running cool or cold potable water except for large, full-thickness burns.
  5. Remove constricting clothing and jewelry before the area swells. If clothing sticks to the burn, do not pull it from the burn but cut around it.
  6. Treat for shock: Have the patient lie down, elevate legs if no trauma and maintain a normal body temperature.
  7. Carefully cover the burn with a nonstick dressing or a sheet over a larger area.
  8. If patient may have inhaled smoke or fumes, watch for airway problems and provide airway and breathing support as needed.
  9. Follow local protocol to administer oxygen if available and you are trained.
  10. Do not apply any cream or ointment on the burn and do not break blisters.
  11. Do not put ice on a burn.
  12. Do not give the patient anything to drink.
  13. Monitor the airway and breathing and be ready to give BLS if needed.
  14. With an infant or a child, a large burn may result in greater fluid and heat loss. Keep the environment warm when possible. With suspicious pattern of burn marks, consider the possibility of child abuse.
