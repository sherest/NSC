Assessing Childbirth Imminence

  1. Ask whether the “bloody show” has occurred.
  2. When did contractions begin? How close together are they and how long does each last? (Time from beginning of one to beginning of the next.) If less than 5 minutes apart and each lasts 45-60 seconds, delivery may occur soon – make preparations. When contractions are about 2 minutes apart, delivery will likely occur soon.
  3. Ask whether the woman feels a strong urge to push or feels like she is having a bowel movement– may mean imminent delivery.
  4. If other signs are suggestive, check for infant’s head crowning. If possible, ask a woman to look. If so, be prepared for delivery very soon – not preparing the woman for transport.
