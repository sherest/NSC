Hazmat Scenes

  1. Approach cautiously from upwind, uphill, or upstream. Stay clear of vapor, fumes, smoke and spills. Keep vehicle a safe distance away.
  2. Secure the scene. Isolate the area and protect yourself and others.
  3. Identify hazards. Look for placards, container labels, shipping documents, etc. Consult a Rail Car and Road Trailer Identification Chart, Safety Data Sheets, and people on the scene.
  4. Assess situation. Consider:
    • Is there a fire, a spill, or a leak?
    • What are the weather conditions?
    • What is the terrain like?
    • Who/what is at risk: people, property, or the environment?
    • What actions should be taken: evacuation, shelter in-place, or dike?  
    • What resources (human and equipment) are required?
    • What can be done immediately?
  5. Obtain help. Contact dispatch to notify responsible agencies and call for assistance from qualified personnel.
  6. Respond in an appropriate manner:
    • Enter area only when wearing appropriate protective gear.
    • Rescue attempts and protecting property must be weighed against you becoming part of the problem.
    • Establish command post and lines of communication.
    • Continually reassess the situation and modify the response accordingly.
    • Consider safety of people in the immediate area first, including your own.

Above all:

Do not assume that gases or vapors are harmless because of lack of a smell – odorless gases or vapors may be harmful. Use caution when handling empty containers because they may still present hazards until they are cleaned and purged of all residues.

Adapted from United States Department of Transportation, 2016 Emergency Response Guidebook.
